No Bs Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 13, 2025

1. Introduction

Most privacy policies are designed to bore you into submission. This one isn’t.

We’ll keep it simple: Synapse: Latency collects some game-related data to improve the experience. No personal details, no invasive tracking - just enough to make sure the world reacts to your choices in meaningful ways.

By continuing to use Synapse: Latency, you agree to this policy. If that sounds ominous, relax, we’re not selling your data to shady third parties.

Now, let’s break this down properly.

2. What Data We Collect

When you interact with the game, we store the following information:

  • Your Choices – Every decision you make is logged (anonymized) to track global player trends and improve narrative branching.
  • Total Player Count – We track how many active players exist to monitor engagement.
  • Notification Settings – Whether you have notifications enabled, so we know if we should send alerts or let you exist in blissful silence.

What We Do Not Collect:

  • Your real name, email, or address.
  • Your browsing history, private messages, or personal files.
  • Any biometric, financial, or legally sensitive data.

The goal is to enhance gameplay, not monitor your personal life.

3. Where Data is Stored and Who Has Access

All data is securely stored and handled through:

  • Cloudflare – Manages security and server protection.
  • Anthem Syndicate – The development team maintaining the game’s backend.

No third-party advertisers, no data reselling, no unauthorized access.

4. What We Actually Do With This Data

  • Choice statistics help us understand how players interact with the story.
  • Player count allows us to measure engagement and server load.
  • Notification settings ensure the system respects your preferences.

That’s it. There’s no behavioral profiling, no targeted ads, and no secret experiments.

However, if we notice that 98% of players make a logical choice while 2% consistently select the worst possible option, we might laugh at the statistics. We won’t know who made the choice—but we will wonder how civilization has lasted this long.

5. Your Rights

If you live in a region with data protection laws, such as the EU (GDPR) or California (CCPA), you have the following rights:

  • Access Your Data – You can request a copy of what we store.
  • Delete Your Data – You can request permanent deletion of your player ID and associated records.
  • Opt-Out of Tracking – You can disable choice tracking, though this may limit access to global statistics.
  • Modify Notification Settings – You can change whether you receive in-game alerts at any time.

To make a request, contact definitelyhuman@anthem-syndicate.com with the subject "Data Request".

We do not make the process unnecessarily difficult, unlike some platforms that require calling customer service just to delete an account.

6. Data Retention

  • Choice statistics are stored indefinitely to power global analytics.
  • Your player ID and notification settings can be removed upon request.
  • Anything unnecessary is automatically discarded.

We do not hoard useless data. Unlike certain megacorporations, we don’t see the value in keeping information forever just because we can.

7. Policy Updates

If we ever change this policy, we will:

  • Announce it via an in-game update log.
  • Not bury it in legal jargon or hide it behind obscure links.

If you continue using Synapse: Latency after an update, you agree to the revised policy. If that ever becomes an issue, you can always request your data to be removed.

8. Contact Information

For questions, data requests, or complaints, contact:

Email: definitelyhuman@anthem-syndicate.com

We actually read messages, so if you need assistance, reach out.

Your data is yours. We're just here to ensure Synapse: Latency remains as immersive as the choices you make.

Now get back to it, someones waiting for ya'.
